Research & Conservation

BeeMonitor Pro provides unprecedented insights into bee behavior and population dynamics.

Native Species Monitoring

Our technology has been optimized for monitoring key native bee species in North America, including:

  • Bombus impatiens (Common Eastern Bumblebee)
  • Bombus griseocollis (Brown-belted Bumblebee)
  • Bombus auricomus (Black and Gold Bumblebee)
  • Osmia lignaria (Blue Orchard Mason Bee)
  • Megachile rotundata (Alfalfa Leafcutter Bee)
  • Xylocopa virginica (Eastern Carpenter Bee)

Data Collection Metrics

  • Colony Activity Patterns: Monitor daily, seasonal, and yearly patterns
  • Environmental Correlations: Link bee activity to weather, temperature, and habitat conditions
  • Population Demographics: Track colony growth and life cycle progression
  • Foraging Behavior: Analyze patterns in pollen and nectar collection
  • Species Interactions: Document competition and cooperation between species
  • Habitat Utilization: Evaluate effectiveness of conservation efforts

Conservation Applications

  • Population Monitoring: Track changes in native bee populations
  • Habitat Assessment: Evaluate effectiveness of restoration efforts
  • Climate Change Impact: Document shifting phenology and range changes
  • Pesticide Exposure: Identify behavioral changes indicating exposure
  • Conservation Planning: Develop data-driven management strategies
  • Public Engagement: Visualize conservation efforts for broader audiences

Selected Research Publications

BeeMonitor Pro technology has contributed to advancing our understanding of pollinator ecology through these recent publications:

Temporal Patterns in Native Bee Activity Across Urban-Rural Gradients

Journal of Urban Ecology, 2024

Continuous monitoring of Bombus species reveals distinct activity patterns between urban, suburban, and rural populations, with implications for conservation planning.

Environmental Factors Influencing Mason Bee Nest Site Selection in Agricultural Landscapes

Agricultural Ecosystems & Environment, 2023

Automated monitoring of Osmia lignaria nesting behavior identifies key environmental factors influencing nest site selection and reproductive success.

Climate Change Effects on Bumblebee Phenology: A Multi-Year Study

Conservation Biology, 2023

Long-term monitoring data shows shifts in emergence timing, colony development, and foraging behavior correlated with climate variables across five years.
